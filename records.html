MAID Expansion — 60,000+ Deaths, Zero RCMP Investigations MAIDFederalLiberal ETHICS 99% 2016–2023 ▼

Canada's Medical Assistance in Dying regime, established by Bill C-14 (2016) and massively expanded by Bill C-7 (2021), produced 60,167+ documented deaths through 2023. Bill C-7 removed the requirement that natural death be "reasonably foreseeable," opened Track 2 for non-terminal patients, and waived final consent requirements. Multiple documented cases show eligibility safeguards were not met (Roger Foley, Alan Nichols, Veterans Affairs Canada incident). UN Special Rapporteurs issued formal communications to Canada. Three RCMP Commissioners — Paulson, Lucki, Duheme — opened zero investigations.

FINDING: 60,167+ deaths. Zero RCMP investigations. UN formal concerns ignored. Safeguard failures documented by Human Rights Watch, disability advocates, and parliamentary committees.
Commissioner Brenda Lucki — Political Interference in Mass Shooting Investigation SCANDALFederal — RCMP ETHICS 99% 2022–2023 ▼

Mass Casualty Commission Final Report (March 2023) found Commissioner Lucki told senior RCMP officers she had promised the PM's Office and Public Safety Minister Bill Blair that the RCMP would release firearms information from the Nova Scotia mass shooting investigation in a way supporting the government's gun control agenda. Chief Supt. Darren Campbell's contemporaneous notes corroborated this. Lucki denied making a "promise" but the Commission found her account less credible. The shooting killed 22 people — Canada's worst mass casualty event.

MCC FINDING (2023): RCMP independence compromised to serve government political agenda. Lucki resigned September 2023. No criminal charges.

Roger Foley — Hospital Staff Offered MAID as Cost-Saving Measure (Recorded) MAIDFederal/Provincial ETHICS 99% 2018–present ▼

Roger Foley, a paralyzed man in London, Ontario, recorded hospital staff suggesting MAID to him while simultaneously denying him adequate home care. The recordings captured a social worker stating it would cost $1,500/day for him to remain in hospital. Foley filed a Charter challenge. Human Rights Watch cited his case in their 2023 report on MAID and disability rights. Despite audio evidence of potential coercion in violation of MAID safeguard provisions, no RCMP investigation was ever opened.

Charter challenge ongoing. Audio evidence of coercion documented. Zero RCMP investigation. Zero charges. Case cited by Human Rights Watch and UN Special Rapporteur.

Alan Nichols, a 61-year-old man with no terminal physical illness, was hospitalized in 2019 for depression. His family reports he was approved for MAID within approximately one month of admission, listing "hearing loss" as his medical condition. His family filed a complaint with hospital authorities. Human Rights Watch documented the case in their 2023 report. Under the law at the time (Bill C-14), MAID required natural death to be "reasonably foreseeable" — his eligibility was disputed. No RCMP investigation was opened.

Documented by Human Rights Watch (2023). Family complaint filed. Zero RCMP investigation. Zero charges. Case contributed to international criticism of Canadian MAID safeguards.
